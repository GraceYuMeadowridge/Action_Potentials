Where can you find free online learning resources?

In the digital age, we can find an astronomical amount of support when acquiring knowledge or skills. At Action Potentials, we have compiled a list of resources that may be useful if you are browsing for a new STEM tutorial series.

Khan Academy is easily the most common platform for students of all ages to practice and expand their mathematical skills. The comprehensiveness of their library is extremely impressive. Its interactiveness allows you to fully synthesize the tutorials, and its user-friendliness by far exceeds other websites. They also offer a variety of other STEM subject topics that mostly follow the Advanced Placement education system.

Coursera is a site where universities create courses for people to take. They are self-paced, free (if you choose to audit it), and offer forums for support. What differs between Coursera and Khan Academy is that Coursera predominantly offers post-secondary level subjects (not restricted to STEM) while Khan Academy targets high school students as their primary customer demography.

If you are interested in coding, check out W3Schools. It offers you documentation, interactive examples, and detailed explanations for almost every topic under or adjacent to the programming language that you are trying to learn.

The Masschusetts Intitute of Technolog created an opensource courseware where students may take self-paced courses and attend lectures hosted by MIT professors. MIT Open Courseware offers introductory post-secondary level courses for students to audit free of charge.

The websites listed above are mostly for following a step-by-step course. Some people may find it structured and helpful, but others may feel restricted by the contents. Therefore, as a supplement, here are some tips when researching for learning resources.

Tip #1: Use open-source textbooks.

There is a rise in the amount of free online textbooks on offer. Partially because of the technological advancements, partially because of its minuscule environmental impacts, online textbooks are becoming more and more popular. Many teachers, professors, and researchers today prefer to self-publish their textbooks to provide easier access to the public. Websites such as Openstax are great sources to download them from and start learning. It will avoid paying hundreds of dollars for a paper textbook, and the contents have just as high a quality as one from a renowned publisher.

Tip #2: Find reliable blogs

Unlike textbooks, blogs are short, neat, and cover all the angles you need to understand a topic. Unlike research papers, they are not as academically focused and are much more comprehensible. Whenever people refer to the issues with research through blogs, they are talking about its reliability. Reliable blogs are some of the best sources of background research because they provide a clear image to readers of all ages. In addition, many blogs nowadays are peer-reviewed by recognized academics, meaning their reliability is just as high as any survey paper published in journals. Do not overlook the power of blogs.

Tip #3: Read scientific magazines and research papers

From a pragmatic perspective, scientific papers are not the easiest to comprehend in comparison to other resources for high school students. However, they offer a unique perspective on the topic that you are researching. Same with scientific magazines, many may offer a contemporary, unparalleled view into a highly academic topic. Textbooks usually take years to publish as they must go through a series of reviews and strict evaluations. Journals also have to be peer-reviewed through criteria just as rigorous as textbooks, except they much shorter and can be published within a few months after the research is done. They are the best way to get on top of contemporary researches and consistently improve the size of your knowledge bank.

Image from (https://lodestar.asu.edu/content/past-research-initiatives)
Tip #4: Dive deep into each topic

If you are researching to learn a topic, dig deep. For example, I personally once read an article that fascinated me so much that I followed its trail of references all the way to the original Latin version of De revolutionibus orbium coelestium, also known as On the Revolutions of Heavenly Spheres by Nicolaus Copernicus. From there, not only I broadened my knowledge of the planetary system, but my Latin reading comprehensive skills also exponentially improved. Therefore, dive deep into a topic, and maybe you will develop a life-long passion for it.

Tip #5: Take notes

Writing things down will allow you to effectively absorb information. It may sound like an old cliche; however, it does hold truth. Even it if cannot help you memorize what you read, you will have a much easier time referring back to it when you come across questions. This is why we take notes in school. Therefore, write down what you read, it will make your life a lot easier down the line.

These are some of my tips and tricks for learning a new STEM subject. In addition to all these sources, refer to our website. At Action Potentials, we offer a variety of learning resources, blogs, and other features for you to discover. As this foundation progresses, there will be more and more resources available to access through our web pages.

At the end of the day, something always holds truth - Take action and reach your potentials!
